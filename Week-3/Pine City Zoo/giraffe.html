The Giraffe is the world's tallest land animal, native to the savannas and open woodlands of sub-Saharan Africa. Known for their long necks and legs, giraffes can grow up to 18 feet (5.5 meters) tall! Their unique spotted coat patterns are as individual as human fingerprints.

ðŸ’¡ Fun Facts:

  • Giraffes' necks can be up to 6 feet long, and they can reach leaves in the tallest trees.
  • Despite their height, giraffes only sleep for 10-30 minutes a day!
  • Their tongues can be up to 18 inches long, helping them grab leaves and twigs from trees.

Giraffes have a peaceful, gentle nature and are often seen grazing in herds across the African savanna.
